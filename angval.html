Conditionals

Zero Conditional First Conditional Second Conditional Third Conditional
Used for present, real/factual situations Used for future, real/factual situations Used for present or future unreal, imaginary situations Used for past unreal, imaginary situations
Present simple + present simple Present simple + will/won't verb Past simple + would + verb Past perfect + would have + past participle
If I stay up late, I feel tired the next day If I stay up late, I will feel tired the next day If I stayed up late, I would feel tired the next day If I had stayed up late, I would have felt tired the next day

Modal Verbs

1. Same form for all subject pronouns
2. No infinitive form "to" after modal verbs (except "ought to")

May, might, could Must Can't
Possible, but not certain Something is certain Something is impossible
This classroom might have a gas leak. The grass must be purple. There can't be a crisis of succession.
